Pre-experiment Information

Understanding the neural basis of recognition

Researcher: Jessica Taubert (School of Psychology, The University of Queensland)

This experiment is one part of a project examining the factors that drive the recognition of objects in our everyday lives.

In this experiment, you will be shown photographs of inanimate objects and asked to make a decision using your mouse. Your speed and accuracy will be recorded. Sitting in the same position for a long period may cause discomfort (e.g., anxiety or a headache). If this occurs, please notify the experimenter.

You may withdraw at any time, without explanation or penalty. Data collected will be de-identified and stored securely. Data that contributes to a publication may be made publicly available via UQ eSpace.

The experiment takes approximately 45 minutes to complete.

This study adheres to the ethical guidelines of The University of Queensland. For concerns, contact Dr. Jessica Taubert at +61 7 3365 7181 or the Ethics Coordinators at +61 7 3365 3924 / +61 7 3443 1656 or email humanethics@research.uq.edu.au.
